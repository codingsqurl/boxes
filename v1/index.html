Who We Are!

Our mission is to provide exceptional tree care services that enhance the safety, health, and beauty of our community. We are committed to preserving Colorado’s natural landscapes through expert solutions, including pruning, storm damage recovery, fire mitigation, and responsible tree removal. With a focus on safety, professionalism, and sustainability, we aim to protect your property while fostering the long-term health of the environment. Owner Paul Hopkins has been in the tree business for twenty years. He was ISA certified in 2009, and started Tree Hoppers in 2017. A native of Colorado Springs, he was born on the Air Force Academy and enjoys the beauty and excitement of our city. He is the fearless leader of crew members Liam and Abel and along with office manager Holly, makes sure that Tree Hoppers continues to be your number one choice for professional tree services in the Southern Colorado area.

Fire Mitigation

Why Fire Mitigation Matters Colorado living means wildfire risk. Mitigation creates
defensible space around your home, protects your neighbors, and keeps you compliant
with local regulations. Plus, many insurers offer discounts—check with your agent.

Storm Damage

Colorado weather hits hard—hail, high winds, and heavy snow can crack, split, or topple trees without warning. Damaged trees aren't just ugly; they're a real danger to your home, power lines, and family. We respond fast to clear fallen trees and remove hazardous limbs before they cause more damage. Whether it's one branch or a whole tree through your fence, we'll get your property back in shape. Need help with an insurance claim? We can document the damage for you.

Pruning

Regular pruning keeps your trees healthy, safe, and looking their best. Dead or overgrown branches can block sunlight, invite disease, and become hazards in storms. We trim strategically to improve structure, boost growth, and protect your property. Whether it's shaping a single tree or maintaining your whole yard, we've got you covered. Healthy trees start with proper pruning.

Removal

Sometimes a tree just has to go—whether it's dead, diseased, or dangerously close to your home. In Colorado, beetle-kill is a major concern: mountain pine beetles have devastated millions of acres, and the outbreak continues to spread along the Front Range. Infested and dead trees aren't just eyesores—they're fire hazards, falling risks, and can spread beetles to healthy trees nearby. We handle removals of all sizes, from tight backyard spaces to large trees near power lines, and we dispose of beetle-kill wood properly to prevent further spread. No stump, no mess, no stress.
